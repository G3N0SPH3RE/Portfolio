# Case Study 1: Automation and Manufacturing Scale-Up

**Capital-Intensive Aerospace Manufacturing Expansion**

**Organization:** Formlogic  
**Role:** Manufacturing and Automation Engineer, Technical Program Lead (On-Site)  
**Program Duration:** August 2021 – October 2022  
**Environment:** Aerospace and defense manufacturing, ITAR, AS9100, ISO 9001

### Program Overview

Formlogic executed a rapid scale-up of precision CNC manufacturing to meet increasing aerospace and defense customer demand. The program combined capital-intensive CNC expansion, robotic automation, and workforce growth while maintaining continuous production and strict regulatory compliance. I acted as the on-site technical program owner, translating executive objectives into executable engineering and manufacturing plans.

### Objectives and Success Criteria

* Increase production capacity while maintaining quality and delivery commitments
* Integrate robotic automation into active, high-precision machining environments
* Maintain ITAR, AS9100, and ISO 9001 compliance during rapid growth
* Improve throughput, utilization, and material flow stability

### My Role and Responsibilities

* Served as on-site technical program lead for automation and manufacturing expansion
* Owned technical scope, execution sequencing, and day-to-day decision-making
* Coordinated electrical, mechanical, controls, CAM, quality, and operations teams
* Partnered directly with executive leadership, including the CEO, on priorities and trade-offs
* Supported procurement strategy, facility layout planning, utilities installation, and vendor coordination

### Technical and Program Challenges

* Scaling capacity while machines were actively producing customer hardware
* Introducing robotics into tight-tolerance aerospace machining workflows
* Balancing aggressive growth targets with compliance and quality requirements
* Aligning global engineering input with on-site execution constraints

### Program Execution and Engineering Approach

* Structured the program into phased rollouts to limit operational risk
* Applied Lean manufacturing methods to identify bottlenecks and sequence improvements
* Designed and implemented custom automation fixtures and PLC-based control systems
* Established RCA and CAPA workflows to stabilize processes during scale-up
* Developed documentation and training materials to support technicians and operators

### Program Budget and Valuation

* Multi-year manufacturing scale-up spanning automation systems, CNC equipment, and customer production throughput
* **Robotic automation:** five industrial robot controllers at approximately $100,000 each, totaling ~$500,000
* **CNC equipment footprint (leased assets, indicative replacement value):**

  * 18 Doosan DVF 5000 vertical machining centers
  * 8 Grob G350 machines
  * 2 DMG Mori DMX U platforms
* **Estimated equipment valuation context (industry typical):**

  * Doosan DVF 5000 class VMCs: ~$350,000–$450,000 each
  * Grob G350 platforms: ~$700,000–$900,000 each
  * DMG Mori DMX U platforms: ~$600,000–$800,000 each
* **Approximate total manufacturing equipment exposure:** on the order of $15M–$20M in CNC assets, excluding automation, facilities, and WIP
* **Engineering and operations staffing:**

  * 3 software and controls developers
  * 1 primary mechanical engineering contractor
  * 2 robotics engineers
  * 2 CAM programmers
  * 5 manufacturing technicians
  * 4 upper management staff, with the CEO actively involved on site
* Overall program value derived from capital equipment exposure, automation investment, and sustained aerospace customer production enabled by scale-up

### Results and Impact

* Increased CNC mill production by approximately 350 percent
* Improved machine utilization, material flow, and scheduling stability
* Reduced defects and rework through controlled process stabilization
* Established a scalable manufacturing and automation foundation for long-term aerospace programs

### Key Takeaways

This program demonstrated the importance of sequencing, risk containment, and technical credibility when leading capital-intensive manufacturing expansions. Acting as the on-site program owner enabled faster decisions, clearer trade-offs, and sustained production during aggressive growth.

# Case Study 2: Robotics System Test and Deployment

**Manufacturing Test Systems for High-Throughput Robotics**

**Organization:** Amazon Robotics  
**Role:** MTAC Project Manager (Project-Based Contract)  
**Program Duration:** May 2024 – October 2024 (6-month contract)  
**Environment:** High-throughput robotics manufacturing and deployment

### Program Overview

Amazon Robotics required accelerated development, validation, and deployment of manufacturing test systems supporting two high-priority robotics programs. These systems acted as production gates for hardware and software integration during manufacturing, making reliability, test coverage, and delivery timing critical to downstream operations.

### Objectives and Success Criteria

* Deliver production-ready test systems for two independent robotics programs
* Validate hardware and software functionality during manufacturing
* Support on-time deployment under aggressive schedules
* Ensure repeatable, reliable test coverage at scale

### My Role and Responsibilities

* Owned system test coordination, evaluation, and deployment readiness
* Led delivery of final production testers used during manufacturing
* Managed four test and engineering staff members across both programs
* Served as the primary interface between engineering, manufacturing, and operations
* Tracked schedules, risks, and readiness for both programs in parallel

### Technical and Program Challenges

* Running two programs concurrently with shared resources
* Delivering test systems that acted as hard manufacturing gates
* Coordinating hardware, software, and vision systems under tight timelines

### Program Execution and Engineering Approach

* Defined test coverage requirements aligned with manufacturing needs
* Prioritized critical-path test functionality to protect delivery dates
* Maintained close coordination with manufacturing to ensure usability and throughput
* Used daily execution tracking and rapid issue escalation to reduce risk

### Program Budget and Valuation

* **Program 1 valuation:** approximately $600,000 for vision and scanning test systems supporting conveyor assemblies and package inspection
* **Program 2 valuation:** approximately $500,000 for vision and scanning test systems supporting inbound and outbound vehicle-based package inspection
* Combined program value of approximately $1.1M, excluding downstream production impact

### Results and Impact

* Delivered production test systems supporting on-time manufacturing ramp
* Reduced integration risk by validating hardware and software earlier in the build process
* Enabled consistent test execution and improved manufacturing confidence

### Key Takeaways

Owning manufacturing test systems reinforced the importance of treating test infrastructure as mission-critical hardware, not support tooling.
